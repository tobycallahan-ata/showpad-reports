How we measured. Every attended, recorded sales call of 10+ minutes tagged with at least one discovery moment in Chorus was included. Discovery depth is the count of distinct dimensions covered — Pain, Goals, Value/Metrics, Decision Criteria, Authority, Budget, Timeline, Next Steps. Progression is measured from Salesforce stage entry dates. Speed is median days between transitions. Win/loss is not yet measurable: the cohort is too young — Build Value progression is the working outcome metric.

1 Discovery is shallow — and evenly shallow — across the funnel

Scoring every substantive call by how many discovery dimensions it covers reveals a strikingly flat, shallow distribution at every stage. Discovery is not being front-loaded in Qualify where it would matter most.

Share of calls by discovery depth band, per stage
Key finding: Roughly 60% of substantive calls at every stage touch only 0–1 discovery dimensions. Qualify calls are no deeper than late-stage Negotiate calls — the window for impactful discovery is being missed.

3 Which discovery dimensions drive progression — and which slow it down

Splitting the cohort by whether each dimension was covered in Qualify isolates what matters. Pain, Value, and Next Steps lift progression. Budget and Authority show flat-to-negative association — and they also make deals measurably slower.

7 Recommended actions

For sales

  • Make pain-probing a deliberate Qualify habit — it is the only signal that increases both rate and speed
  • Aim to cover Pain + Timeline + Value as the non-negotiable core of every first call
  • Sequence Budget and Authority conversations after pain is established — early BANT does not help and drags deals
  • When the prospect voices decision uncertainty, slow down and re-establish value before advancing

For enablement

  • Adopt Pain coverage rate as the primary coachable KPI on Qualify calls
  • Target ≥4 discovery dimensions covered per Qualify call as the quality bar
  • Investigate the 5 high-ARR Q2 deals with zero Chorus coverage — confirm calls are being recorded
  • Re-run this analysis in Q3 2026 as the cohort matures — win/loss will become measurable

Caveats

  • Association, not causation. Engaged prospects naturally surface more discovery and progress further; depth is partly a marker, not purely a cause.
  • Short, censored window. Tracker history begins Feb 2025; win/loss is not yet measurable and deeper-stage hops are directional only.
  • Cohort is Qualify-filtered. Every deal already reached Qualify — this measures progression from Qualify, not whole-pipeline risk.
  • Speaker attribution. Moment signals are not speaker-scoped in this view — the PDF's analysis separated rep technique from buyer signals.
  • Chorus coverage gaps. Deals with zero calls may have unrecorded or external calls; absence of Chorus data ≠ absence of activity.

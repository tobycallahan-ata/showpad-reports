01 — Executive Summary

The case for restructuring

Showpad holds 939 active assets across 21 channels. YTD engagement (Jan–May 2026) totalled 4,161 events from 111 unique users — but is declining sharply (–69% from February to May) and heavily concentrated in a single user. The top 25 assets absorb the vast majority of interactions, while 23% of active assets have never been viewed at all. The taxonomy has a real foundation (81.6% tagged) but critical gaps: 37% of assets have no audience label and 88% have no buying-stage tag. Sellers navigate a structure built around content ownership (who made it) rather than intent (what a seller needs right now). This document proposes collapsing 21 channels into 7, consolidating the tag taxonomy, and designing around the seller's buying-stage workflow — not the org chart.

The core finding

Showpad is currently organized the way the content creators think about their work — by department, by product line, by team. But sellers don't think that way. They think: "I have a call with a financial services prospect tomorrow who is in late-stage evaluation. What do I need?"

The current structure forces them to browse 21 channels and guess which one holds the right asset. The usage data tells the story: only 111 unique users engaged with Showpad in the first five months of 2026, and engagement has been declining sharply since February's RKO peak. When Showpad is hard to navigate, sellers route around it: ad-hoc Slack requests, local file saves, and Google Drive links replace governed, tracked content sharing. A structural rebuild around seller intent will reverse this.

03 — Root Cause Analysis

Six structural problems

The usage data, hygiene audit, and channel map all point to the same root causes. These are structural — not content quality — problems.

1

Organised for creators, not consumers

The 21-channel structure maps to the Ataccama org chart (Sales Eng, Customer Success, RevOps, Legal, etc.), not to seller workflow. A seller preparing for a discovery call must already know which internal team owns the content they need.

2

Tag taxonomy exists but is fragmented

81.6% of active assets are tagged — a real foundation. But 235 unique tags is too many: the taxonomy includes typos (Anomalo), CDAta), event-specific tags (RKO, SKO, FWRD 2025, Q1 2024), and currency codes (GBP, USD, CAD) that add noise without value. More critically: 37% of active assets have no audience label (External/Internal), and 88% have no buying-stage tag — the two dimensions sellers need most.

3

Too many channels, too many sections

21 channels and 132 sections create decision paralysis. Navigation studies consistently show that users abandon browsing beyond 7±2 top-level options. With 21, sellers default to the two or three channels they already know.

4

No clear "freshness" signal

Outdated content (Feb 2024 pitch decks, 2024 battlecards, old playbooks) sits alongside current material with no visual differentiation. Sellers can't tell if what they're downloading is the approved current version.

5

23% of active assets have never been viewed

218 of 939 active assets have zero lifetime views. Not all are candidates for immediate archival — some are new uploads awaiting promotion, others are niche reference docs. But a significant portion are old enablement material, newsletters, and product intros from 2023–2024 that were never surfaced and are now stale. A triage process (see §7) is needed rather than a blanket archive.

6

Engagement is declining and concentrated in one user

YTD engagement peaked in February (RKO period) at 1,477 events, then fell 69% to 455 events by May. One user (Frank Waldenburger) accounts for 22% of all YTD interactions — meaning the apparent "usage" significantly flatters the true adoption picture. Without structural change, May's numbers may become the baseline.

⚠️ The adoption risk

When sellers can't find content in Showpad within 90 seconds, they route around it. The engagement data supports this inference: only 111 unique users in the first 5 months of 2026, engagement declining sharply after RKO, and the top 25 assets absorbing the vast majority of interactions. A platform that isn't being used for regular deal prep is not delivering its intended value — and untracked sharing (Slack links, local copies, Drive exports) means content governance breaks down at the point of customer contact.

The current structure trains sellers to not rely on Showpad. A rebuild around seller intent reverses that.

04 — Design Principles

What the rebuild is optimised for

Before defining the new structure, these are the non-negotiable principles that guided the recommendation. Any future changes to the structure should be evaluated against them.

🎯

Intent over ownership

Structure content around what a seller is trying to accomplish — not which team created it.

⚡

Findable in < 90 sec

A seller who knows roughly what they need should never spend more than 90 seconds finding it.

🏆

Fewer, better choices

5–7 channels. Curated sections. One clearly-approved version of each key asset. No sprawl.

📅

Freshness is visible

Sellers should be able to see at a glance whether an asset is current. Governance enforces this.

🏷️

Tags unlock everything

Tags (not channel position) are the long-term organising system. Channels are just curated views into tagged content.

🔒

Separate internal from external

Content sellers use themselves vs. content they share with prospects should never be confused.

06 — Tag Taxonomy

Build on what exists — fix the gaps

Contrary to the initial draft of this report, Showpad does have a tagging foundation: 766 of 939 active assets (81.6%) carry at least one tag. The problem is not absence — it is fragmentation. 235 unique tags is too many, critical dimensions are undercovered, and 173 active assets (all primarily SE recordings) have no tags at all. The work is consolidation and gap-filling, not a rebuild from scratch.

⚠️ What the data actually shows

Audience visibility gap (37%): 347 of 939 active assets have no External/Internal label. Sellers genuinely cannot tell what is safe to share with a prospect. This is the most urgent tagging fix.

Stage coverage gap (88%): The stage dimension already exists — tags like Qualify, Build Value, Develop Solution, Negotiate are in use and map directly to Ataccama's SFDC stages — but only 112 of 939 active assets (11.9%) carry any stage tag. The structure is right; the coverage is not.

Taxonomy noise: 235 unique tags includes typos (Anomalo), CDAta), event-specific tags that expire (Q1 2024, RKO, SKO, FWRD 2025), currency codes (GBP, USD, CAD), and onboarding week markers (Week 1–4). These add search noise and erode trust in the system.

173 untagged SE recordings: The highest-value gap. Several of these have 100–250 views yet carry zero tags — including assets like SE-E Lineage intro w Feri.mp4 (248v), SE-E Processing Pushdowns.mp4 (184v), and SE-E MS Fabric.mp4 (173v).

Recommended tag clean-up: three actions, not a rebuild

1. Deprecate 32 noisy tags (full list below). Remove or merge event-specific tags (Q1 2024, RKO, SKO, FWRD 2025), fix typos, and retire currency codes and week markers. Target: ≤180 tags total.

2. Close the audience gap. Add External – public, External – NDA confidential, or Internal only to the 347 unlabelled active assets. This is the highest-risk gap — a seller sharing an Internal only doc because it had no label is a real compliance exposure.

3. Expand stage coverage, SFDC-aligned. Retire "Validate" as a standalone tag; fold its assets into "Develop Solution" (where POC validation sits in Ataccama's process). Apply the five-stage taxonomy (Qualify → Build Value → Develop Solution → Propose & Negotiate → Retain & Expand) to the 827 stage-untagged assets during Phase 2 migration — content stewards tag their sections as they migrate. Multi-stage assets (e.g. pricing) get tagged for all stages where they're used.

07 — Content Lifecycle

Superseded assets: what to action now

The 218 never-viewed active assets are not a single homogeneous problem. Before bulk archiving, they need triage. Below is the current evidence-based breakdown, including the confirmed superseded pairs identified from the hygiene report.

⚠️ Don't archive all 218 never-viewed assets at once

Some never-viewed assets are new uploads not yet promoted. Some are niche reference materials with a narrow audience who knows where to find them. A blanket archive run would destroy legitimate value. The right approach is a three-tier triage:

🔴 Archive immediately
Never viewed AND created before 2025 AND not a reference doc. Estimated: ~80–100 assets. Includes old newsletter PDFs, superseded enablement materials, expired event assets.
🟡 Triage with steward
Never viewed but created 2025–2026, or reference-type content. Steward confirms: still relevant → promote; no longer relevant → archive. Estimated: ~80 assets.
🟢 Promote actively
Never viewed but high-quality (e.g. recent vertical pitch decks for less-covered industries, new case studies). These need a Slack announcement or homepage pin, not archival. Estimated: ~40 assets.

08 — Content Governance

How to keep the structure clean: freshness strategy

The biggest risk to any Showpad restructure is not the initial migration — it is the gradual accumulation of new stale content that recreates the current problem within 18 months. Freshness needs to be a system, not an annual panic.

📅

Naming convention signals age

Year in asset names is already common practice ([MASTER] 2025 Licensing, 2026 Gartner MQ). Enforce it: every customer-facing deck must include the year. Old-year decks are immediately visible without needing to look at metadata.

🏷️

Freshness tags, not dates

Add three system tags: Current (2026), Needs Review, and Archive Candidate. Stewards apply these during quarterly reviews. Smart filters can then show sellers only "Current" assets if they want a freshness-filtered view.

⏰

Automated hygiene alerts

The existing asset hygiene report script flags assets not updated in 90+ days with <5 lifetime views. Schedule this report monthly and route it to content stewards — they own their channel's freshness, not just its initial population.

Content lifecycle policy (proposed)

StateCriteriaTagTrigger
Active — Current Updated within 12 months OR >5 views/month Current (2026) Steward confirms at quarterly review
Active — Needs Review Not updated in 90 days AND <5 views in last 30 days Needs Review Monthly hygiene report flags; email to steward
Archive Candidate Not updated in 180 days AND <5 views lifetime OR confirmed superseded Archive Candidate Steward reviews and approves within 30 days or asset is archived
Archived Steward or admin action; soft-delete in Showpad — Can be restored within 90 days if needed

✅ What already exists to support this

The scripts/asset_hygiene_report.py script already surfaces "Stale" assets (not updated in 180+ days, <10 lifetime views) and "Never Viewed" assets with a hygiene chip per row. Running it monthly and routing the output to stewards is the primary enforcement mechanism. A scheduled run can be added via the automation toolkit with no additional development.

The "Needs Review" and "Archive Candidate" tag states can be applied directly in Showpad or in bulk via the asset API — making the lifecycle process scriptable, not dependent on manual Showpad UI clicks.

09 — Implementation

A 90-day roadmap

The rebuild should happen in three phases. Phase 1 focuses on what sellers see immediately; Phase 2 on the structural cleanup; Phase 3 on governance that keeps it clean. None of these phases require Showpad downtime — all are additive until the cutover in Phase 2.

1

10 — Expected Outcomes

What success looks like — and how to measure it

Success is not a one-time state — it is a set of signals that confirm the rebuild is working and a governance system that keeps it working. Below are the target outcomes, the metrics that track them, and the reports needed to surface those metrics.

📈

Reverse the engagement decline

Current: 455 events in May 2026 — down 69% from February. Target: ≥700 events/month by end of Q3 2026 and ≥900 by Q4. Grow the 111 unique YTD users and improve regularity of use — more users returning weekly, not just at RKO. Report: Monthly YTD engagement report (existing script); add a unique-user-per-month trend line.

⏱️

Sub-90-second content retrieval

Homepage pinboard + 4 clear seller channels + consolidated tags means a seller knows exactly where to look. Proxy measure: session duration in Showpad analytics. Short sessions on specific assets = found it fast. Long, multi-channel sessions = still searching. Track the ratio.

🗑️

Active library reduced by ~30–40%

Triaging 218 never-viewed assets (archive ~40%, promote ~20%, triage ~40%) plus superseded pairs reduces the active pool toward ~560–660. Report: Asset hygiene report run monthly; track active asset count trend over time. Smaller, curated library → more trust.

🏷️

Close the tagging gaps — not start from zero

Baseline today: 81.6% tagged, but 37% missing audience label and 88% missing stage tag. Targets: 100% audience-labelled (all active assets carry External/Internal tag), 50%+ stage-tagged by end of Phase 2, 70%+ by Phase 3. Report: Add a "tag coverage by dimension" summary card to the hygiene report — audience label %, stage tag %, product tag %.

🔄

Reduced ad-hoc "where's the deck?" requests

A leading qualitative signal that navigation is working: fewer Slack asks in #sales and #presales for specific assets. Establish a pulse check: survey the AE/BDR team 30 days post-cutover with two questions: "Did you find what you needed?" and "Did you use Showpad or another source?"

📊

Measurable content ROI by buying stage

Once stage tags are applied, SFDC + Showpad share data can answer: which assets are used in won vs. lost deals? Which stage content correlates with faster close? New report needed: "Asset engagement by stage tag" — a pivot of the YTD event data against the stage tag dimension. This does not currently exist; build it in Phase 3.

The bottom line

The tagging foundation already exists at 81.6% — this is not a rebuild from scratch, it is gap-filling and consolidation. The channels need collapsing from 21 to 7. The homepage needs to surface what sellers actually reach for. And the governance system needs to exist so that the structure stays clean in 18 months without another full restructure.

The content is largely good. The engagement data shows sellers know which 15–20 assets they want. The rebuild's job is to surface those assets instantly, build a durable structure around seller intent rather than org ownership, and instrument the system so content ROI is measurable — not just assumed.
